Vitroscribe

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 26, 2026

Introduction

Vitroscribe ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. Vitroscribe is a privacy-first macOS application designed to transcribe meetings entirely on your local device. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your data when you use our application and connect to third-party services like Google Calendar and Microsoft Outlook.

1. Data Processing (Privacy-First Architecture)

Critical Privacy Note: Vitroscribe is designed so that your sensitive data never leaves your computer.

  • Audio & Transcripts: All audio recording and speech-to-text transcription are performed entirely on your Mac using WhisperKit with Apple Core ML. No audio, transcript, or speech data is ever uploaded to our servers or any third-party cloud.
  • Multilingual Processing: The on-device Whisper model supports transcription in multiple languages. All language processing happens locally with no network calls.
  • Local Storage: Your transcripts and meeting history are stored in a local SQLite database on your device's hard drive. We have no access to this data.
  • Auto-Record: When Auto-Record Meetings is enabled, recordings start automatically when a meeting is detected. No audio data leaves your device regardless of this setting.

2. Google API Data Usage

When you connect your Google Calendar, Vitroscribe requests read-only access to your calendar events.

  • How we use this data: We fetch upcoming events to identify meeting links (Google Meet, Zoom, Teams) and display them in your dashboard and menu bar. This allows the app to provide the "Join & Capture" HUD and upcoming meetings list.
  • Storage: We do not store your Google Calendar data on any external servers. This data is only held in memory and locally on your device to facilitate app features.
  • Data Sharing: We do not sell, trade, or share your Google user data with any third parties.

Google Limited Use Disclosure: Vitroscribe's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Service User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

3. Microsoft API Data Usage

When you connect your Microsoft account, Vitroscribe requests read-only access to your Outlook calendar events via Microsoft Graph.

  • How we use this data: We fetch upcoming events to identify Microsoft Teams meeting links and display them alongside your Google Calendar events.
  • Storage: We do not store your Microsoft calendar data on any external servers. Data is only held locally on your device.
  • Data Sharing: We do not sell, trade, or share your Microsoft user data with any third parties.

4. Permissions

Vitroscribe requires the following permissions to function:

  • Microphone: To capture meeting audio for local transcription.
  • Screen Recording / Window List: To detect meeting application windows (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, Slack, Webex) on your screen. No screen content is captured or stored — only window titles and application names are read.
  • Automation: To read browser tab titles and URLs via AppleScript, used solely to detect active Google Meet, Zoom, or Teams tabs. No browsing data is stored or transmitted.
  • Calendar: To sync your schedule (via Google/Microsoft OAuth).

5. Third-Party Services

Vitroscribe uses Google and Microsoft OAuth for calendar authentication. These services operate under their own privacy policies. Vitroscribe does not use any analytics, crash reporting, or advertising services.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date.
